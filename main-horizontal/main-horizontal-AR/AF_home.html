Online Trading

Online trading is a fairly popular method of transacting stocks online. Traditionally, when a buyer wants to invest money in stocks, he calls his brokerage firm and asks for putting in a request to buy stocks of a given company for a specified amount.

The broker would then let him know the market price of the stocks and confirm the order.

After the user confirms his trading account, the broker's fees and the time period required for the order, the order gets placed on the stock exchange

As obvious, this method had multiple steps, online trading platforms have taken over the entire trading experience, when a user places an order for buying a particular stock on an online platform, the order gets saved in the database of the trading platform and the exchange platform. If the price matches the order gets executed.

Online platforms provide a far more inexpensive, self-learning experience. It is faster & more accurate, which attracts a majority of traders and investors

Over the Counter (OTC)

The transfer of ownership mechanism of unlisted securities through the OTC mechanism; This is the mechanism through which the pre-arranged deals are executed.

Over the counter refers to the trading of securities outside of a formal exchange and with the supervision of the exchange regulator.

Companies trading on exchange are called listed companies unlike companies that trade OTC, they are referred to as unlisted companies.

Delivery versus payment (DVP)

DVP is a settlement method that guarantees the transfer of securities only happens upon payment.

The process is meant to reduce the risk that stocks could be delivered without payment or that payments could be made without the delivery of stocks.

Margin Lending

Margin lending is a type of loan that allows you to borrow money from the brokerage company to invest by using your existing shares as a security.

Margin lending is not recommended to beginners, only professional trader and experienced clients.

The amount you can borrow is based on your net portfolio position whether cash or shares.

If the value of your security drops in relation to the loan amount, This will trigger a margin call and you'll be required to reduce your loan amount or sell part of your investment

MCSD Listing

Central Depository activity refers to any activity related to the depository of securities, clearing and settlement of financial positions resulting from trading transactions and the relevant pledge of securities

Short Selling

Short selling is a trading strategy where the position is opened by borrowing shares of a stock that the investor believes will decrease in value, then sells them to buyers willing to pay the market price.

The investor will later purchase the same position in order to return them to the lender. If the price has fallen in the meantime, the investor will have made a profit equal to the difference.

On the contrary if the price has risen in the same period, a loss will be incurred.

Same Day Trading

Same day trading refers to buying then selling or selling short then buying the same security on the same day.

Same day traders depend on short-term trading strategies & intraday technical calls to capitalize on small price movements that occur in highly liquid stocks.

Among the most popular techniques for same day trading is trading based on the news such as scheduled announcements of economic statistics, corporate earnings and interest rates

Technical Analysis

Technical analysis along with fundamental analysis are ways used to determine the direction of a stock, hence estimating a target price. It is used to identify trading opportunities by analyzing statistical trends gathered from trading activity, such as price movement and volume.

This kind of analysis believes that past trading activity and price changes of a security can be valuable indicators of the security's future price movements.
